Some statistics:

> The global average atmospheric carbon dioxide concentration has risen to about 407 ppm in 2018 (primarily due to increased fossil fuel usage), which is the highest it's ever been at any point in the last 800,000 years.

> A 2008 study found that for every degree Celsius the temperature rises due to CO2 levels, ozone pollution is expected to kill an additional 22,000 people through respiratory illness, asthma, and emphysema.

> The average passenger car produces almost one pound of carbon dioxide per mile travelled.

> For a typical American houshold with two cars, car transportation solely accounts for 47% of their carbon footprint.

> An average American household spends $1,075 on various goods per month, which equates to an average of 12 tons of carbon dioxide a year.

> The production of beef requires roughly 20 times as much land, and emits 20 times as many emissions compared to beans, per gram of protein. It even requires 10 times as much when compared to chicken through this same metirc.

How the calculator works:

This calculator is rudimentary estimate of your carbon footprint based on transport and diet, for people in the USA. References for my data are located below.

PSA: This assumes electric cars emit ZERO pounds of CO2 a year, however this varies depending on the car, model, location, and also doesn't take into account the carbon used in the production of the cars.
